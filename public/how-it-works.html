Process

How ClusterSEO Pro Works

From a raw keyword list to a structured content strategy in four simple steps — no technical knowledge required.

1

Upload or Paste Your Keywords

Start by giving ClusterSEO Pro your raw keyword data. You can paste keywords directly into the manual input field — one per line — or upload a CSV or TXT file exported from any keyword research tool. If your keywords have search volume data, include it in the format keyword, volume and we'll use it in the analysis.

Pro Tips

  • Export your full keyword list from Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Google Keyword Planner first
  • Include search volume data to get traffic estimates per cluster
  • You can paste a list of 500+ keywords at once — no limit
2

Adjust Clustering Sensitivity

Use the sensitivity slider to control how strictly keywords are grouped together. Set it to Loose to create broad topical clusters — great for pillar pages and topic hubs. Set it to Tight to create very specific clusters — perfect for planning individual landing pages or blog posts.

When to use each setting

  • Loose (20–40%): Building topical authority clusters and content pillars
  • Medium (45–55%): Standard keyword grouping for most blog/page planning
  • Tight (60–80%): Creating granular, highly-specific page targeting
3

Run the Clustering Analysis

Click "Start Clustering Analysis" and our engine goes to work. Every keyword is analyzed for its underlying search intent using linguistic signals and SERP pattern recognition. The result is four clean groups: Informational, Transactional, Commercial, and Navigational — each one telling you exactly what content to create.

What happens under the hood

  • Keywords are scanned for intent-signal words (how, buy, best, login, etc.)
  • SERP similarity patterns are matched to group related keywords
  • Volume data is aggregated per cluster automatically
4

Review, Filter & Export Results

The results tab shows you all four intent clusters with keyword counts and total search volume. Click any intent type card to drill into its keywords and see the full list in a sortable table. When you're ready, export a clean CSV to share with your content team, or copy everything to your clipboard in one click.

What to do next

  • Map each cluster to a specific page or content piece in your plan
  • Use Commercial clusters for comparison/review pages with high conversion potential
  • Use Informational clusters to build topic authority and answer key questions in your niche
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ClusterSEO Pro really free?
Yes, completely free. No account, no credit card, no hidden limits. You can analyze as many keywords as you need at no cost.
How many keywords can I process at once?
There is no hard limit. The tool handles lists of hundreds or even thousands of keywords in a single pass. For very large lists, uploading a CSV file is recommended over manual pasting.
What file formats are supported for upload?
CSV (.csv) and plain text (.txt) files are supported. The expected format is one keyword per line, with an optional comma-separated volume value. A header row like "keyword,volume" is automatically detected and skipped.
How accurate is the intent classification?
The classifier uses a combination of intent-signal keywords, SERP pattern matching, and linguistic analysis. For most standard keyword lists it performs very accurately. Complex or ambiguous keywords may occasionally be classified differently than a manual expert review — use it as a starting point and refine as needed.
Can I export the results?
Yes. You can export a structured CSV containing each keyword, its search volume, and its intent category. You can also copy all results to your clipboard in a formatted text layout.
Do I need technical knowledge to use this tool?
Not at all. If you can copy and paste a list of keywords, you can use ClusterSEO Pro. The interface is designed to be intuitive for SEO beginners and professionals alike.
